Front page

Internet DJ Console is a project started in March 2005 to provide a powerful yet easy to use source-client to individuals interested in streaming live radio shows over the Internet, targetting the Shoutcast and Icecast server-platforms.

In addition to providing most of the features a person could want when producing a live radio show this software is written with the aim of producing the best experience for listeners and DJ alike.

Features like VoIP integration involve the listeners who are often part of tight-knit communities on the net, while others like the DJ alarm, and the silence-killer help ensure that shows run smoothly.

Incorporating the technology of Jack Audio Connection Kit, while frustrating for some, offers IDJC the ability to extend its audio capabilities beyond what would otherwise be possible through the use of specialized external programs, naming Skype, Jack Rack (audio sound effects) and Jamin (the powerful audio compressor/equalizer) though, the list of applications that could be connected is extensive and create interesting potential.

Fortunately all this power does not come at the expense of a user interface resembling a messy desk, nor have requests for features been permitted to diminish the applicaton's intuitive feel however throughout development the main targets have always been that of stability and audio quality and your listeners may thank you for it (in IRC of course).

Stephen Fairchild
